Year of 2021-22 Newsletter

Chapter Status

The past school year has been an exciting return to normalcy for the chapter, as we returned to in person classes, and to our chapter's continued growth. Including the most recent Upsilon pledge class, there are 112 active brothers, 26 of which will graduate this spring.


Current Executive Board

In the Fall, we elected our new executive board. Our executive board consists of the roles of President (Kenji Hoang), VP Internal (Alec Pool), VP External (Nick Greco), Secretary (Marco Casareto), Treasurer (Abhinav Vedmala), Social Chair (Jack Humphreys), and Risk Manager (Evan May).

Serenades

This year marked our first full year as members of the IFC since 2017, and we made the most of it. We took part in Homecoming Week in the fall, where we were paired with GPhi and DU. In the spring, we participated in Greek Week, where we paired with SK and LTA.

Homecoming and Greek Week

In addition to taking part in major IFC events, we performed serenades for 4 sororities in the fall, and plan to do 6 total in the spring. These serenades helped us make valuable connections, and led to socials and bar tabs with a number of new sororities.

Our Tau class (pictured) performed "Brandy (You're a fine girl)" by Looking Glass, "Ain't No Mountain High Enough" by Marvin Gaye, and "You Belong With Me" by Taylor Swift.

Our Upsilon class performed "Grenade" by Bruno Mars, "Classic" by MKTO, and "Fireflies" by Owl City.

Homecoming and Greek Week

This fall, we took part in Homecoming, and were paired with Gamma Phi Beta sorority and Delta Upsilon fraternity. We painted pumpkins, picked up trash, and enjoyed a number of social events.

For Greek Week, we were paired with Sigma Kappa and Lambda Theta Alpha Sororities.

Brotherhood Events

In addition to spending more time getting to know the rest of Greek life, we saved time for one another, too. In the fall, we went to TopGolf, and won some great intramural soccer games. We also had brothers go skydiving. In the spring we played Paintball and established a new tradition of visiting Dave and Busters.

Trips

Over the past year, brothers have traveled far beyond College Park. Cancun, Miami, Puerto Rico, are among the destinations, along with The Outer Banks and Pennsylvania Mountains for Away week and Weekend.

Spring Break: Cancun

A group of 18 brothers visited Cancun during spring break, along with nearly as many guests and friends.

Away Week and Weekend

In the fall, brothers went to visit Lake Harmony in the Poconos. In a few weeks, we will be taking a trip to the Outer Banks of North Carolina. While not every brother can make it, many consider it the highlight of their semester.

Philanthropy and Community Service

A core tenet of Sigma Phi Delta's mission is duty to those around us. Throughout the year, we have worked with a number of charities, in some cases in partnership with the Greek community.

Fuck Cancer Event

We co-sponsored an event with multiple sororities and fraternities on campus. The event was hosted at Soundcheck and had a live DJ.

Meals on Wheels

We partnered with Meals on Wheels to aid in distributing produce to families in need in the DC area. We also helped Meals on Wheels in its operations by helping to paint a room to be used for administrative purposes.

Meals on Wheels

We are currently selling hoodies to raise money for Ukrainian Children that were affected by the war. All profits will go straight to Tabeltochki, hoodies can be purchased at https://sigphi.typeform.com/ukraine.

Gamma Phi Moonball

Our team won Gamma Phi's moonball tournament in support of Girls on the Run, Gamma Phi's national philanthropy event.

Academic Development

Scholarship remains an important facet of our brotherhood. Being in an Engineering and Computer Science fraternity offers our brothers a unique opportunity to study alongside their closest friends. Despite the challenge and rigor of an Engineering or Computer Science course load, brothers earned an average GPA of 3.38, outperforming the UMD average of 3.35 and the all-fraternity average of 3.26, and placing us as the third highest GPA for an IFC fraternity. In the fall, our Tau pledge class had the second chapter GPA of 3.4, while being among the largest pledge classes in the IFC. A special shoutout goes to brother Matthew Patrick, who earned his Master's Degree in Human Computer Interaction this Spring.

Professional Development

In addition to working to find academic success, our chapter places a premium on Professional Development. We have run a number of events designed to help brothers find professional opportunities that suit them, and have continued to work to collect professional resources. Brothers this semester found continued professional success, and will be working and interning all across the country next year. In addition to many of us staying in the DC/Maryland/Virginia (DMV) area, multiple graduating brothers will be working in New York and Seattle, and some will be in Boston, San Antonio, and Madison. Younger brothers have also found plenty of their own success. This summer, in addition to the DMV area, Sig Phi will also be represented in San Francisco, Huntsville, Menlo Park, and Boston, among other places.
